A Look at Disney's Wait Times

By: Abigail Roedersheimer, J352


Disneyland's Main Street on a crowded day. Credit: OC Register

For many families, a trip to Disney can be a once-in-a lifetime vacation. Which explains why wait times have reached records of up to 8 hours, and the company is able to make millions on fast-passes, now dubbed Lightning Lanes.

Using numbers gathered from Thrill Data, which compiles wait times for attractions at parks around the nation every five minutes, Disneyland had a 24-minute average wait time in 2024 and its comparable park in Florida, Disney World’s Magic Kingdom, fell slightly lower, at 20 minutes. This could be due to a number of factors, including Magic Kingdom’s 22-acre advantage over the Anaheim park’s.

Averages were calculated by taking average wait times for all attractions from the 15th of every month at half-hour intervals. While these averages may be a far cry from what most park-goers may remember waiting in, it's because they include times from less popular attractions, such as the Mad Tea Party, which took just 12 minutes on average to clear at Disneyland in 2025.

On a monthly basis, Disneyland’s most lengthy month is July, and Disney World’s most lengthy month is February. Outside of these peaks, waits follow average temperatures, increasing with warmer weather.


Throughout the day at Disneyland, wait times grow as morning progresses, with slight dips at typical meal times such as 12 pm and 5:30 pm, and decrease as guests begin to leave for the day.

Disney World’s day is slightly less predictable, with waits growing until 12, then falling off until 3:30. It then reaches its peak average wait time at 6:30, before quickly shrinking. This difference could be attributed to the wider variety of park options Orlando guests have access to, or a broader offering of night shows, which draws guests in later in the day.

Overall, Disney Parks are not unfamiliar with guest aversion to lines. A $60 billion, 10-year investment in expanding Disney World, which will likely disperse guests across more space and attractions, was announced in early 2024. The project includes a Cars Land in Magic Kingdom, replacing Tom Sawyer’s Island, which had an average wait time of just five minutes in 2024.
